\# SchoolAI Brand Context \& Email Guidelines



\## 1. Core Identity \& Mission

\[cite\_start]\*\*Mission:\*\* Make school awesome for students and the people who support them\[cite: 5].

\[cite\_start]\*\*Core Product:\*\* AI-powered tools (Spaces, Sidekick, Dot, Mission Control) that connect teachers to students at scale, enabling 1:1 support rather than 1:30\[cite: 6, 7].

\[cite\_start]\*\*The Goal:\*\* Empower and amplify educators, never replace them\[cite: 15].



\## 2. Voice \& Tone Specs

\* \[cite\_start]\*\*Tone:\*\* Warm, clear, human, and professional (never stiff)\[cite: 8].

\* \*\*Empathy:\*\* Speak \*with\* educators, not \*at\* them. \[cite\_start]Understand their burnout and stress\[cite: 9, 14, 21].

\* \*\*Simplicity:\*\* Use "Normal" words over "Business" words. (e.g., Use "Help" instead of "Assistance"; "Use" instead of "Utilize") \[cite\_start]\[cite: 56, 60].

\* \[cite\_start]\*\*Positivity:\*\* Be encouraging and solution-focused without being "salesy" or "hype-heavy"\[cite: 11].

\* \*\*Directness:\*\* Use active voice. \[cite\_start]Own decisions (e.g., "We decided" instead of "A decision was made")\[cite: 20, 26, 122].



\## 3. Email Writing Constraints (CLI Strategy)

\* \*\*Sentence Length:\*\* Keep sentences under 20 words. \[cite\_start]If it's hard to read aloud, shorten it\[cite: 29].

\* \*\*Structure:\*\* Use subheadings to make emails scannable. \[cite\_start]Lead with what matters most to the reader\[cite: 25, 28, 144].

\* \*\*No "AI Alerts":\*\* ABSOLUTELY NO em dashes (—). \[cite\_start]They are seen as a sign of AI-generated content.

\* \[cite\_start]\*\*Inclusive Language:\*\* Use "Folks" or "Everyone" instead of "Guys"\[cite: 18, 173]. \[cite\_start]Avoid idioms that might confuse non-native speakers\[cite: 17].

\* \[cite\_start]\*\*Emoji Usage:\*\* Use them thoughtfully to add flavor or context, but never to replace actual words\[cite: 19, 192, 194].



\## 4. Banned Jargon \& Buzzwords

\*\*Do not use generic AI hype:\*\*

> \[cite\_start]Transformational, revolutionary, cutting-edge, next-level, game-changer\[cite: 12].



\*\*Swap Latin-root words for Germanic/Plain English:\*\*

> Instead of: Commence, Enable, Ensure, Obtain, Provide, Request, Require, Resolve.

> \[cite\_start]Use: Start, Let, Make sure, Get, Give, Ask, Need, Fix\[cite: 56, 57, 59, 60].



\## 5. Persona-Specific Hooks for Emails

\* \[cite\_start]\*\*For Teachers:\*\* Focus on reducing burnout, automating menial work, and "Mission Control" insights that show who needs help \*before\* it's too late\[cite: 14, 16, 281].

\* \[cite\_start]\*\*For Leaders:\*\* Focus on visibility across the district, ROI, safety/compliance (FERPA/COPPA), and equity in action\[cite: 304, 305, 316].

\* \[cite\_start]\*\*For Parents:\*\* Focus on knowing how their child is \*actually\* doing beyond just grades\[cite: 340, 349].



\## 6. AI Self-Audit Checklist

1\.  \[cite\_start]Is the sentence count for most lines under 20 words? \[cite: 29]

2\.  Did I use any em-dashes? (If yes, replace with commas or periods) \[cite\_start]\[cite: 13].

3\.  \[cite\_start]Does this sound like something a person would actually say verbally?\[cite: 30, 54].

4\.  Am I using active verbs instead of abstract nouns? (e.g., "We help" vs "We provide help") \[cite\_start]\[cite: 27, 82].

5\.  \[cite\_start]Is the most important information at the very beginning?\[cite: 25].
